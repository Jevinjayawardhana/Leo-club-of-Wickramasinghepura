Project Diriya Diwimaga 3.1


On 20th December, Diriya Diwimaga 3.1 was successfully carried out at the National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH), Angoda, focusing on menstrual dignity and hygiene for women under institutional care.

This meaningful initiative was jointly organized by the Leo Club of Wickramasinghepura in collaboration with the Leo Club of Athugalpura, addressing an often overlooked yet essential need with compassion and care.

On 20 th December
National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH) - Angoda

Project උත්තමාචාර 1.1


On 21st December, we successfully carried out Project උත්තමාචාර 1.1 at Shiloh Elders Home, Hokandara, with a focus on Senior Citizens Development and well-being.

The initiative included cleaning and uplifting the living environment, followed by a meaningful spiritual session conducted by a Buddhist monk, creating a calm and positive atmosphere for them. The day concluded with an evening of togetherness, where snacks were shared and heartfelt moments were enjoyed with the elders.

This project was a reminder that care, respect and compassion can make a lasting difference in the lives of senior citizens.

On 21 st December
Shiloh Elders Home - Hokandara

Leo Genesis 1.1


Leo Genesis 1.1 is a Betterment of Leoism initiative aimed at strengthening Leo values and inspiring personal and leadership growth among Leos.

Phase 1 was implemented as a digital video series featuring Leos from club, district, and council levels who shared their personal journeys in Leoism. The videos highlighted what Leoism means to them and how it has contributed to their leadership development, career growth, academic balance, skill enhancement, and personal transformation, while also inspiring current and aspiring Leos.

The project created a platform for knowledge sharing, motivation, and connection, reaching a wider audience through digital platforms. With the successful completion of Phase 1, Leo Genesis continues with Phase 2, focusing on further leadership development and holistic growth of Leos.

Project Wave of Support 1.2


Project Waves of Support 1.2 was a compassionate three-day humanitarian relief initiative conducted to assist communities severely affected by the Dhitwa Cyclone. The project focused on meeting urgent basic needs by distributing essential dry food items such as rice, soya meat, biscuits, milk powder, and safe drinking water. In addition, sanitary and hygiene items including soap and Dettol were provided to help prevent health risks. Through timely support, the project aimed to restore dignity, safety, and hope among affected families.

On 29 th and 31 stDecember
Sirasa TV Depanama / Badulla Area
